Aqua Dubai — Monthly Performance · Hutong Dubai

June 2026 (full calendar month) · AED · MoM vs May 2026 · YoY vs June 2025 · one venue
The read. June held its ground where it counts. Completed covers were 2,831 (−4% on May, −24% YoY), but completed revenue was flat month-on-month at AED 907k because spend per cover rose to AED 321 — guests are fewer but spending more. The year-on-year gap is a footfall story, not a conversion one: web conversion rose to 7.3% (from 6.5% last June) and the shortfall sits almost entirely in walk-ins as post-conflict footfall stays soft. Digital is doing the holding: the reservations team (38% of covers) and the website widget (27%) are now the two load-bearing channels. Paid media spent AED 29.9k at a blended AED 93 per booking — Meta improved its efficiency, but Google's non-brand search remains expensive and is the clearest fix for July.

PlanExpect July to be the softest month. Hold paid spend steady rather than chasing volume into a seasonal trough, fix the Google non-brand cost-per-booking, keep the reservations team and website widget well-supported, and be ready to push again into the September recovery.

7 · What's working / what's not

Working

  • Revenue held flat MoM on higher spend per cover (AED 321) — demand per guest is strong.
  • Web conversion up YoY (7.3% vs 6.5%) and cancellations down to 9.5% — a cleaner, higher-quality book.
  • Meta efficiency improved: flat spend vs May, CPA down to AED 103, strong remarketing (AED 46–67).
  • Google brand: AED 65 per booking at 28% CTR — the single most efficient line in the account.
  • Reservations team + website widget now carry 65% of covers, absorbing the walk-in loss.

Not working / watch

  • Walk-ins remain the YoY hole as post-conflict footfall stays soft — covers −24% YoY.
  • Google non-brand search: AED 4,877 at AED 129–363 per booking — the clearest cost lever.
  • Two Meta prospecting/remarketing reels running at AED 188–257 CPA — pause or refresh.
  • MoM conversion normalised from May's 8.8% high; traffic growth isn't yet converting to more bookings.
  • Seasonal downturn ahead — July is the softest month; don't over-spend into the trough.
