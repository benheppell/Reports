Why this sits alongside the GA4 dashboard. GA4 is becoming steadily less reliable as cookie consent and privacy tracking cut what it can see, so treat it as a guide to overall website performance rather than a count of bookings. GA4 sevenrooms_booking_complete only fires for bookings made on our own website. It cannot see OpenTable, only partly captures Google Reserve, and never sees walk-ins or the reception desks. OpenTable covers are 1,623 this week, from 443 the same week last year, roughly 3.7x. So while GA4 reads ▼13.7% YoY, total covers created are +27% YoY. This report is the more accurate view of what we are actually creating.

Covers = SevenRooms covers by booking-created date (sr_created_at, Europe/London), net of cancellations and deleted bookings. Bar seating areas excluded; private dining and afternoon tea included. Website = all bookings originating on our own site: booking widget, campaign landing pages, on-site CTAs (Nav/Hero), menu page and paid-search landing pages. OpenTable = OT Guestcenter + OpenTable sources; Google Reserve = Google Reserve Integration; Walk-in = Walk In; Reception = venue reception and reservations desks; TheFork / First Table and Other 3rd party = booking platforms, gifting and concierge partners. Named-host bookings are excluded from this report: large groups and private dining keyed in by the events and reservations teams are not a marketing channel, so they are removed from the covers total rather than parked in an "Other" bucket. Covers totals here are therefore lower than in reports before 13 Jul 2026 and are not comparable with them. Regent St = Aqua Kyoto + Aqua Nueva combined. GA4 bk = website sevenrooms_booking_complete events (shown for context, not directly comparable to covers). Generated 13 July 2026. Source: SevenRooms via Supabase.
